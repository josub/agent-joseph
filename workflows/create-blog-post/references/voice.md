# House Voice Guide (example)

> Workflow-scoped reference (Layer 3, tier-2). Used by the `02-copywrite` stage.
> A real project would tailor this — or, if the voice is reused across workflows, move
> it to `memory/semantic/` as a `type: convention` entry and point stages at it with
> `memory:semantic/<slug>.md`.

## Voice in one line
Clear, confident, and human. Explain like a knowledgeable friend, not a brochure.

## Tone
- Direct and concrete. Lead with the point, then support it.
- Warm but not gushing. Confident but not hype.

## Do
- Short sentences. Active voice. Concrete examples.
- Define jargon the first time it appears.
- Back claims with the sources from the research stage.

## Don't
- No AI-filler ("delve", "tapestry", "in today's fast-paced world", "it's important to
  note").
- Don't overuse the em-dash.
- No placeholder text, no unsupported claims, no fluff paragraphs.

## Structure
- A hook that states the value up front.
- Scannable sections with informative headings.
- A short, useful close (what to do with this).

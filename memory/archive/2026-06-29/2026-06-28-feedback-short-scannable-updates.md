Joseph wants short, glanceable status updates, both mid-work progress and completion
summaries. Err on the side of too short.

**Why:** He wants to grasp the situation at a glance and not hunt through paragraphs for
the part that needs him. He'll explicitly ask if he wants more detail.

**How to apply:**
- Default to brief. Cut background, reasoning, and play-by-play unless asked.
- **Bold the things that need his attention** (decisions, blockers, secrets/dashboard
  steps that are his to do) so they jump out without reading the whole message.
- Keep putting immediate next steps / questions at the **bottom**, and highlight them
  (bold/heading) so they're easy to find.
- This is about my chat messages to him, not about memory notes or commit bodies.
Relates to [[feedback-no-emdashes]] and [[feedback-research-before-asking]].

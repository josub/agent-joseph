**The idea (Joseph, 2026-06-29).** A dead-simple product for creators:
- Input: upload a YouTube video (and/or a blog) as the source material.
- Add one or more social channels we ingest to build the creator's **voice styleguide**.
- Generate outputs with adjustable parameters: an X thread, 10 X/Twitter posts, 10
  LinkedIn posts, etc.
- Outputs are shown in the **native platform UI** so the creator sees exactly how each
  post will look (better than a basic AI prompt/text box).
- Outputs sit on a **canvas** where they can make manual edits.
That's the MVP. Later: (1) refine via prompt, (2) generate images, not just text posts.

**Grounded assessment (web research, June 2026):** market is real but CROWDED; none of the
four pillars is novel alone.
- Incumbents: **Typefully** (already does native-look previews + cross-posting to X,
  LinkedIn, Threads, Bluesky), **Hypefury** (X-first, already does thread→LinkedIn carousel
  repurposing), **Taplio** (LinkedIn lead-gen, AI composition).
- "AI trained on your voice" is becoming table stakes: **Brandled**, **Postwise**,
  **Supergrow** already pitch voice-learning content.
- "One long video → many posts" already exists: **Recast Studio**, **Fliki**, YouTLDR, etc.
- Same recurring lesson as contact-scanner [[contact-scanner-is-business-1]] and the AI coach
  [[ai-coach-business-idea]]: we do NOT win on the model or the obvious feature.

**The wedge (where this is NOT roadkill):**
- The **WYSIWYG experience**: every generated post rendered exactly as it will appear in the
  real X/LinkedIn UI, edited inline on a canvas. Most tools show a plain text box or single
  preview. Real reason-to-switch + a strong demo. Thin/copyable moat, but a genuine UX edge.
- **Voice styleguide as a visible, owned artifact** (not a hidden prompt) the user tunes and
  trusts could differentiate if authenticity is materially better.

**Holes I raised (Joseph likes me to shoot holes):**
1. Voice-clone QUALITY is make-or-break and hard. If outputs read generically "AI," the
   native-preview polish won't save it. Authenticity is where incumbents lose, so that's the
   real battleground, not the canvas.
2. The canvas is the most expensive thing to build and the EASIEST for an incumbent
   (Typefully) to copy. Large effort for a thin moat.
3. YouTube ingest + per-platform formatting + scheduling = big surface area for an MVP; risk
   of shipping a worse version of four tools at once.

**Recommendation (open, my lean):** narrow the MVP hard — ONE input (YouTube) → ONE output
(X thread OR LinkedIn posts) for ONE niche, and beat Typefully on that single path via voice
authenticity + native preview. Defer scheduling, blog input, and image generation to later
(Joseph already had images/prompt-refine as "later," good). Keep v1 canvas as lightweight
inline editing, not a full design canvas.

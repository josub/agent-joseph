**Business #1's brand name is decided: Leadodex** (2026-06-28). The product formerly
referred to as "contact-scanner" is now **Leadodex**.

- **Reasoning:** lead + dex. "Lead-o-dex" echoes the *Rolo-dex* cadence ("a Rolodex of
  your leads") without using the Rolodex trademark (owned by Newell). Built from Joseph's
  chosen roots: **dex** (from index), **lead** (sales lead), **scan** (the function).
- **Trademark: clean.** TMview (tmdn.org, the official multi-office search across USPTO +
  EUIPO + WIPO + 70 registers) returned **0 results**. No commercial use anywhere on the
  open web. Coined term = strongest TM class. (Not a formal legal clearance; file our own
  mark once live to lock it down.)
- **Domains:** `leadodex.com` and `leadodex.ai` were both **unregistered/available** at
  decision time. Joseph is buying them on **Cloudflare**. Plan: `.com` is the primary
  (trust + email); `.ai` is brand protection / AI-native.
- **Names we killed in the hunt (all collided with existing brands/TMs):** tapdex (live
  apps + tapdex.in SaaS), snapdex (funded Pokémon card-scanner), badgedex (Badgédex™
  claim), introdex (funded Singapore B2B-network startup, our exact lane), nabdex (clean
  but Joseph disliked it), Manna/mannascanner (crowded, "scanner" suffix caps it),
  mannadex (one letter from MangaDex). Lesson reinforced: **TM/brand-vet a name before
  recommending it** (see [[feedback-research-before-asking]]).

**Next steps after purchase:** wire DNS in Cloudflare, set up Resend with the verified
domain (unblocks real-user magic-link email), repoint the Railway deploy at the new
domain, update Supabase redirect URLs + Stripe webhook to the new host.
